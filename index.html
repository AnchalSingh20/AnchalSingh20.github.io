About

About me

I am a Ph.D. scholar in the Department of Information Technology at the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Allahabad (IIIT-A), where I conduct research in Safe Artificial Intelligence (Safe AI). My work focuses on developing AI systems that are secure, robust, interpretable, and trustworthy, with an emphasis on understanding and improving the reliability of modern deep learning models.

My research is supported by the UGC–NET Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and is supervised by Prof. Krishna Pratap Singh and Dr. Naveen Saini. I am particularly interested in advancing Safe AI through principled approaches that combine robustness, explainability, and trustworthy machine learning for real-world applications.

Before joining IIIT Allahabad in 2024, I completed my B.Sc. (Hons.) and M.Sc. in Computer Science from Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi.

Research

Research areas

My research lies at the intersection of Safe AI, adversarial machine learning, computer vision, and trustworthy AI. I investigate how deep learning models behave under adversarial conditions and develop methods to improve their security, robustness, and interpretability.

01

Adversarial Machine Learning

Understanding the vulnerabilities of deep neural networks by designing and analyzing adversarial attacks under white-box and black-box threat models.

02

Adversarial Defenses

Developing defense mechanisms that improve the robustness and reliability of CNNs, Vision Transformers, and foundation models against adversarial manipulation.

03

Explainable & Trustworthy AI

Studying explanation drift, attention behavior, and feature representations to understand model reasoning and improve transparency under adversarial conditions.

04

Vision Foundation Models

Evaluating the robustness and safety of CNNs, Vision Transformers, and vision-language foundation models for real-world and safety-critical applications.

Research Vision
“

My long-term research vision is to develop AI systems that are not only accurate but also secure, interpretable, fair, and dependable. I am particularly interested in advancing Safe AI by designing robust learning algorithms that can withstand adversarial manipulation while providing transparent and trustworthy explanations for their decisions.
